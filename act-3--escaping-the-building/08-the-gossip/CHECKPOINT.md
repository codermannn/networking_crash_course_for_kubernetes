# Checkpoint: Module 08 · The Gossip

> **Feynman Self-Assessment Checklist**
> Ask yourself these questions and see if you can explain them in simple terms (no jargon) to a colleague.

---

### 1. What is an Autonomous System (AS)?
*If a colleague says: "The internet is just one big network of routers," how do you explain the boundaries? What is an AS, and who owns them?*

---

### 2. How does BGP (Border Gateway Protocol) gossip work?
*Use the "clerk gossip" analogy. When a router says: "I know a path to Google," how does it prove the route? What is the AS Path?*

---

### 3. What is a BGP Hijack, and why is it so easy to execute?
*Explain the 2008 Pakistan/YouTube incident. Why did the entire planet follow the lie? What does this tell you about the trust model of the internet?*

---

### 4. How does BGP routing differ from the static routing we configured in Module 07?
*If a wire cuts under the Atlantic, how does BGP heal the path without human intervention?*

---

### Checkpoint Rule:
If you can explain at least **3 out of 4** of these questions confidently, you have mastered global routing. You are ready to step into Act IV: how do we hold a conversation once we reach the room?
